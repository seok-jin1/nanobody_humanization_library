# Computational Stability Analysis of Nanobody Humanization Mutations

## Abstract

This study presents a computational analysis of 13 framework mutations required for humanizing an anti-FAP nanobody through CDR grafting onto the human IGHV3-66 germline framework. Using Rosetta Cartesian ddG calculations on an AlphaFold3-predicted structure, we systematically evaluated the thermodynamic impact of each mutation to identify potentially destabilizing substitutions prior to experimental validation.

---

## Materials and Methods

### 1. Nanobody Sequence and Target Framework

#### 1.1 Source Nanobody
The starting nanobody sequence (125 residues) was derived from an anti-FAP (Fibroblast Activation Protein) single-domain antibody:

```
QVQLQESGGGSVQAGGSLRLSCAASGYTVRSSYMGWFRQVPGKQREAVAIITSGGTTYYADSVKGRFTISRDNAKNTLYLQMNSLKPEDTAMYYCAGRTGFIGGIWFRDRDYDYWGQGTQVTVSS
```

#### 1.2 Humanization Strategy
Humanization was performed through CDR grafting onto the human IGHV3-66 germline framework. Framework mutations required for humanization were identified using the IMGT numbering scheme for antibody sequences, with exclusion of VHH hallmark residues (IMGT positions 42, 49, 50, 52) and conservation of essential structural elements including the disulfide bond at Cys22-Cys95. A total of 13 framework mutations were identified across four framework regions (FR1-FR4).

### 2. Structure Prediction

Three-dimensional structure prediction was performed using AlphaFold3 (Jumper et al., 2024). The nanobody sequence was submitted to the AlphaFold3 server, generating five structural models (models 0-4). Model 0 was selected for downstream analysis based on its high confidence score (pTM = 0.88), exact sequence length match (125 residues), proper disulfide bond formation between Cys22 and Cys95, and absence of steric clashes or disordered regions. The selected structure was obtained in mmCIF format (`fold_anti_fap_nb_model_0.cif`).

### 3. Mutation Mapping and Preparation

#### 3.1 IMGT to Sequential Numbering Conversion
Framework mutations were initially identified using IMGT numbering, which includes gaps and insertions. A custom Python script (`01_fix_imgt_numbering.py`) was developed to map IMGT positions to sequential PDB residue numbers based on direct sequence alignment, framework region-specific offsets (FR1: ~0, FR2: ~3, FR3: ~9, FR4: ~0), and manual verification of each mutation position.

#### 3.2 Mutation List Preparation
The final mutation list (`01_mutations_final.txt`) was formatted for Rosetta input:
```
total 13
1
Q 1 E
1
Q 5 V
1
S 11 L
[...]
```

**Note**: An initial error in mapping IMGT position 12 (S→L) to sequential position 7 was corrected to position 11 after sequence verification.

### 4. Cartesian ddG Calculations

#### 4.1 Software
Rosetta macromolecular modeling suite (version 2026.06) was used for all energy calculations. Specifically, the `cartesian_ddg` application was employed for high-accuracy mutation energy predictions.

#### 4.2 Calculation Protocol
Cartesian ddG calculations were performed using the following protocol:

```bash
cartesian_ddg.linuxgccrelease \
  -in:file:s ../01_structure_prediction/fold_anti_fap_nb_model_0.cif \
  -ddg:mut_file 01_mutations_final.txt \
  -ddg:iterations 5 \
  -ddg:cartesian \
  -ddg:bbnbrs 1 \
  -ddg:dump_pdbs false \
  -score:weights ref2015_cart \
  -fa_max_dis 9.0 \
  -ddg:legacy true
```

This produces `02_mutations_final.ddg` (raw energy output, 14 × 5 = 70 lines).

#### 4.2.1 Set2: Position Saturation (I55, P96)
For positions where the initial humanization mutation was destabilizing (I55V: +2.52 REU, P96A: +3.44 REU), alternative amino acids were tested:

```bash
cartesian_ddg.linuxgccrelease \
  -in:file:s ../01_structure_prediction/fold_anti_fap_nb_model_0.cif \
  -ddg:mut_file 01_mutations_set2.txt \
  -ddg:iterations 5 \
  -ddg:cartesian \
  -ddg:bbnbrs 1 \
  -ddg:dump_pdbs false \
  -score:weights ref2015_cart \
  -fa_max_dis 9.0 \
  -ddg:legacy true
```

This produces `02_mutations_set2.ddg` (raw energy output, 11 × 5 = 55 lines).
Set2 mutations: I50→G/A/S/Y/R/F/L/Q (8 variants at IMGT 55) + P87→V/D (2 variants at IMGT 96).

Key parameters included the ref2015_cart score function (a Cartesian-space energy function optimized for high-resolution refinement), 5 independent iterations per mutation for statistical confidence, backbone flexibility of ±1 residue from the mutation site (bbnbrs 1), a repacking radius of 9 Å (fa_max_dis 9.0) for sidechain repacking, Cartesian coordinate space (more accurate than torsion space), and legacy mode for compatible output format with downstream analysis tools.

#### 4.3 Computational Details
For each mutation:
1. Wild-type (WT) baseline structure was relaxed using FastRelax protocol
2. Mutant structure was generated by substituting the target residue
3. Local relaxation was performed (backbone ±1 residue, sidechains within 9 Å)
4. Energy was scored using ref2015_cart score function
5. Process was repeated 5 times with different random seeds

Total calculations: 14 structures (WT + 13 mutations) × 5 iterations = 70 calculations
Computational time: Approximately 4 hours on a single Intel Xeon CPU core

### 5. Data Analysis

#### 5.1 ddG Calculation
For each mutation, the change in folding free energy (ddG) was calculated as:

```
ddG = E_mutant - E_WT
```

where E_mutant and E_WT represent the average Rosetta energy (in REU) over 5 iterations.

Standard deviation was calculated from the 5 iterations to assess convergence:

```
σ_ddG = sqrt(σ²_mutant + σ²_WT)
```

#### 5.2 Classification Criteria
Mutations were classified based on ddG values (in Rosetta Energy Units):

| Classification | ddG Range (REU) | Interpretation |
|----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Strongly destabilizing | > +2.0 | High risk for expression/stability |
| Moderately destabilizing | +1.0 to +2.0 | Medium risk, consider reverting |
| Mildly destabilizing | +0.5 to +1.0 | Low risk, monitor |
| Neutral | -0.5 to +0.5 | Minimal impact |
| Stabilizing | < -0.5 | Beneficial for stability |

**Note on REU units**: Rosetta Energy Units are dimensionless and approximately correlate with kcal/mol (1 REU ≈ 0.5-1.0 kcal/mol), but this conversion is approximate. The Pearson correlation coefficient between Cartesian ddG and experimental ddG is typically R ~ 0.6-0.7.

#### 5.3 Statistical Confidence
Mutations with standard deviation > 1.0 REU were flagged as having lower confidence and may require additional iterations for better convergence.

## Discussion

### 4.1 Framework Region Sensitivity
The FR2 region exhibited particular sensitivity to mutations, with 3 out of 4 mutations (75%) classified as strongly destabilizing. This suggests that the FR2 region plays a critical structural role in this nanobody and may be less tolerant to humanization mutations.

### 4.2 Proline Effects
Two proline-related mutations showed opposing effects. A14P (proline introduction) was highly destabilizing (+6.04 REU), consistent with reduced backbone flexibility, while P87A (proline removal) was also destabilizing (+3.44 REU), though this may be overestimated due to known Cartesian ddG artifacts with proline substitutions.

### 4.3 Unexpected Stabilization
**G35S** (ddG = -1.38 REU) showed stabilizing effects despite initial predictions of destabilization from loss of glycine flexibility. This suggests that the serine sidechain may form favorable local interactions.

### 4.4 Most Favorable Mutation
**S11L** (ddG = -3.54 REU) was identified as the most stabilizing mutation, though the high standard deviation (2.95 REU) suggests this estimate has lower confidence and may benefit from additional iterations.

## Recommendations

Based on computational predictions:

### Phase 1: Conservative Humanization
Based on computational predictions, we recommend retaining 7 out of 13 mutations (Q5V, S11L, G35S, A74S, K86R, M92V, Q120L) while reverting 6 mutations identified as high-risk (Q1E, A14P, V40A, A49S, I50V, P87A).

### Phase 2: Experimental Validation
The conservative humanization variant should be generated and characterized by measuring expression yield and stability (Tm, aggregation propensity). If necessary, medium-risk mutations can be systematically tested and compared with the full humanization variant containing all 13 mutations.

### Phase 3: P87A Verification
Given potential artifacts in proline calculations, P87A should be evaluated separately through molecular dynamics simulations and experimental mutagenesis studies to confirm the computational predictions.

---

## Limitations

1. **Rosetta ddG accuracy**: Correlation with experimental ddG is R ~ 0.6-0.7; predictions should be validated experimentally
2. **Static structure**: Calculations based on single AlphaFold model; conformational dynamics not considered
3. **Solvent effects**: Implicit solvation model; explicit water molecules not included
4. **Proline artifacts**: Known limitations in Cartesian ddG for proline substitutions
5. **High standard deviations**: Three mutations (A49S, K86R, S11L) show σ > 1.0 REU, indicating lower convergence

---

## Conclusions

Computational analysis successfully identified 6 high-risk mutations out of 13 framework substitutions required for nanobody humanization. The conservative humanization strategy (retaining 7 safe mutations) is predicted to maintain structural stability while achieving partial humanization. Experimental validation is required to confirm computational predictions and optimize the final humanized variant.
